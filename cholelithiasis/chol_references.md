# Cholelithiasis (Gallstone Disease) — Curated QSP References

> Compiled for the QSP mechanistic model. Includes foundational papers on bile acid physiology,
> biliary cholesterol transport, pharmacology (UDCA, statins, ezetimibe), and clinical endpoints.
> PubMed links are provided for each entry.

---

## 1. Epidemiology & Disease Burden

1. **Lammert F, Gurusamy K, Ko CW, et al.**
   *Gallstones.* Nat Rev Dis Primers. 2016;2:16024.
   PMID: [27121804](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/27121804/)
   > Comprehensive primer covering epidemiology, pathophysiology, genetics, and treatment.

2. **Stinton LM, Shaffer EA.**
   *Epidemiology of gallbladder disease: cholelithiasis and cancer.* Gut Liver. 2012;6(2):172-187.
   PMID: [22570746](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/22570746/)
   > Prevalence ~10-15% in Western adults; risk factors and geographical variation reviewed.

3. **Shaffer EA.**
   *Gallstone disease: Epidemiology of gallbladder stone disease.* Best Pract Res Clin Gastroenterol. 2006;20(6):981-996.
   PMID: [17127183](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/17127183/)
   > Annual incidence and population-based prevalence data.

4. **Peery AF, Crockett SD, Murphy CC, et al.**
   *Burden and cost of gastrointestinal, liver, and pancreatic diseases in the United States: Update 2018.*
   Gastroenterology. 2019;156(1):254-272.
   PMID: [30315778](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/30315778/)
   > US GI disease burden; gallstones remain leading cause of GI hospitalization.

## 2. Pathophysiology & Bile Composition

5. **Admirand WH, Small DM.**
   *The physicochemical basis of cholesterol gallstone formation in man.*
   J Clin Invest. 1968;47(5):1043-1052.
   PMID: [5648605](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/5648605/)
   > Seminal paper introducing the triangular phase diagram (CSI framework); molar % bile acids, PC, cholesterol.

6. **Wang DQ, Cohen DE, Carey MC.**
   *Biliary lipids and cholesterol gallstone disease.*
   J Lipid Res. 2009;50 Suppl:S406-S411.
   PMID: [19109234](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/19109234/)
   > Review of the interplay between cholesterol, phospholipid, and bile acid secretion.

7. **Carey MC, Small DM.**
   *The characteristics of mixed micellar solutions with particular reference to bile.*
   Am J Med. 1970;49(5):590-608.
   PMID: [4990786](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/4990786/)
   > Phase boundaries for micellar cholesterol solubilization in bile.

8. **Holzbach RT, Marsh M, Olszewski M, Holan K.**
   *Cholesterol solubility in bile. Evidence that supersaturated bile is frequent in healthy man.*
   J Clin Invest. 1973;52(7):1467-1479.
   PMID: [4711954](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/4711954/)
   > Shows that supersaturated bile occurs physiologically; nucleation time distinguishes stone formers.

9. **Donovan JM, Carey MC.**
   *Physical-chemical basis of gallstone formation.*
   Gastroenterol Clin North Am. 1991;20(1):47-66.
   PMID: [2022413](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/2022413/)
   > Crystal nucleation kinetics, vesicle-to-micelle transition, and mucin scaffold role.

## 3. Bile Acid Metabolism & FXR/FGF19 Regulation

10. **Chiang JY.**
    *Bile acids: regulation of synthesis.*
    J Lipid Res. 2009;50(10):1955-1966.
    PMID: [19346331](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/19346331/)
    > CYP7A1 regulation by FXR-SHP-HNF4α axis; bile acid feedback control.

11. **Inagaki T, Choi M, Moschetta A, et al.**
    *Fibroblast growth factor 15 functions as an enterohepatic signal to regulate bile acid homeostasis.*
    Cell Metab. 2005;2(4):217-225.
    PMID: [16213225](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/16213225/)
    > FGF15/19 ileal-to-hepatic feedback suppresses CYP7A1 via FGFR4-β-Klotho.

12. **Lefebvre P, Cariou B, Lien F, Kuipers F, Staels B.**
    *Role of bile acids and bile acid receptors in metabolic regulation.*
    Physiol Rev. 2009;89(1):147-191.
    PMID: [19126757](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/19126757/)
    > Comprehensive review of FXR, TGR5, VDR, PXR in bile acid signaling.

13. **Lu TT, Makishima M, Repa JJ, et al.**
    *Molecular basis for feedback regulation of bile acid synthesis by nuclear receptors.*
    Mol Cell. 2000;6(3):507-515.
    PMID: [11030331](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/11030331/)
    > FXR-SHP-CYP7A1 feedback loop at molecular level.

14. **Hofmann AF, Hagey LR.**
    *Bile acids: chemistry, pathochemistry, biology, pathobiology, and therapeutics.*
    Cell Mol Life Sci. 2008;65(16):2461-2483.
    PMID: [18488143](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/18488143/)
    > Master reference for bile acid chemistry, enterohepatic recycling, and pool size.

## 4. Hepatic & Intestinal Cholesterol Transport

15. **Berge KE, Tian H, Graf GA, et al.**
    *Accumulation of dietary cholesterol in sitosterolemia caused by mutations in adjacent ABC transporters.*
    Science. 2000;290(5497):1771-1775.
    PMID: [11099417](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/11099417/)
    > Identification of ABCG5/ABCG8 as obligate heterodimers for biliary/intestinal cholesterol efflux.

16. **Yu L, Hammer RE, Li-Hawkins J, et al.**
    *Disruption of Abcg5 and Abcg8 in mice reveals their crucial role in biliary cholesterol secretion.*
    Proc Natl Acad Sci USA. 2002;99(25):16237-16242.
    PMID: [12461182](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/12461182/)
    > Knockout mice have markedly reduced biliary cholesterol; validates ABCG5/8 as therapeutic target.

17. **Trauner M, Fickert P, Wagner M.**
    *MDR3 (ABCB4) defects: a paradigm for the genetics of adult cholestatic syndromes.*
    Semin Liver Dis. 2007;27(1):77-98.
    PMID: [17295178](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/17295178/)
    > MDR3/ABCB4 mutations reduce biliary phospholipid, increasing lithogenicity.

18. **Mauad TH, van Nieuwkerk CM, Dingemans KP, et al.**
    *Mice with homozygous disruption of the mdr2 P-glycoprotein gene: a novel animal model for studies of nonsuppurative inflammatory cholangitis and hepatocarcinogenesis.*
    Am J Pathol. 1994;145(5):1237-1245.
    PMID: [7977650](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/7977650/)
    > mdr2 (MDR3 mouse homolog) knockout: absent biliary PC, spontaneous liver disease.

19. **Altmann SW, Davis HR Jr, Zhu LJ, et al.**
    *Niemann-Pick C1 Like 1 protein is critical for intestinal cholesterol absorption.*
    Science. 2004;303(5661):1201-1204.
    PMID: [14976318](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/14976318/)
    > NPC1L1 as intestinal cholesterol transporter; ezetimibe target identified.

## 6. UDCA Pharmacology & Stone Dissolution

23. **Bachrach WH, Hofmann AF.**
    *Ursodeoxycholic acid in the treatment of cholesterol cholelithiasis.*
    Dig Dis Sci. 1982;27(8):737-761. [Part I & II]
    PMID: [7094810](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/7094810/)
    > Landmark review: UDCA mechanism, clinical dissolution rates, recurrence.

24. **Jazrawi RP, Pazzi P, Petroni ML, et al.**
    *Postprandial gallbladder motor function: refilling and turnover of bile in health and in cholelithiasis.*
    Gastroenterology. 1995;109(2):582-591.
    PMID: [7615211](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/7615211/)
    > GB bile turnover kinetics during postprandial period; validated UDCA dosing TID > QD.

25. **Petroni ML, Jazrawi RP, Pazzi P, et al.**
    *Risk factors for the development of gallstone recurrence following medical dissolution.*
    Eur J Gastroenterol Hepatol. 2000;12(6):695-700.
    PMID: [10872586](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/10872586/)
    > 30–50% recurrence over 5 years; identifies predictors (large stones, multiple stones).

26. **Paumgartner G, Beuers U.**
    *Ursodeoxycholic acid in cholestatic liver disease: mechanisms of action and therapeutic use revisited.*
    Hepatology. 2002;36(3):525-531.
    PMID: [12198643](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/12198643/)
    > UDCA mechanisms: hepatoprotection, immunomodulation, FXR partial agonism, TGR5 activation.

27. **Ward A, Brogden RN, Heel RC, Speight TM, Avery GS.**
    *Ursodeoxycholic acid: a review of its pharmacological properties and therapeutic efficacy.*
    Drugs. 1984;27(2):95-131.
    PMID: [6368266](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/6368266/)
    > Comprehensive pharmacological review of UDCA including PK parameters used in model calibration.

28. **Villanova N, Bazzoli F, Taroni F, et al.**
    *Gallstone recurrence after successful oral bile acid treatment: a 12-year follow-up study and evaluation of long-term postdissolution treatment.*
    Gastroenterology. 1989;97(3):726-731.
    PMID: [2670182](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/2670182/)
    > Long-term UDCA and maintenance therapy to reduce recurrence.

## 8. Genetic Determinants

32. **Buch S, Schafmayer C, Völzke H, et al.**
    *A genome-wide association scan identifies the hepatic cholesterol transporter ABCG8 as a susceptibility factor for human gallstone disease.*
    Nat Genet. 2007;39(8):995-999.
    PMID: [17632509](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/17632509/)
    > GWAS identifies ABCG8 D19H variant (rs11887534) as major gallstone susceptibility locus.

33. **Wang HH, Portincasa P, Wang DQ.**
    *Molecular pathophysiology and physical chemistry of cholesterol gallstones.*
    Front Biosci. 2008;13:401-423.
    PMID: [17981555](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/17981557/)
    > Lithogenic gene Lith1 (Abcg5/Abcg8 locus) and its interaction with diet.

34. **Katsika D, Grjibovski A, Einarsson C, Lammert F, Lichtenstein P, Marschall HU.**
    *Genetic and environmental influences on symptomatic gallstone disease: a Swedish study of 43,141 twin pairs.*
    Hepatology. 2005;41(5):1138-1143.
    PMID: [15841454](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/15841454/)
    > Heritability of gallstone disease ~25%; environmental factors dominant.
