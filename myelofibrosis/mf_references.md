# Myelofibrosis QSP Model — References

Comprehensive bibliography for Quantitative Systems Pharmacology (QSP) modeling of Myelofibrosis (MF), covering epidemiology, molecular pathophysiology, bone marrow fibrosis, hematopoiesis disruption, clinical assessment tools, JAK inhibitor pharmacology, pivotal clinical trials, treatment guidelines, QSP/PK-PD modeling, and emerging therapies.

---

## 1. Epidemiology & Natural History

### Incidence and survival of myelofibrosis in the United States, 2001–2012
**Authors**: Srour SA, Devesa SS, Morton LM, et al.  
**Journal**: *Leukemia* 2016;30(9):1945–1948  
**PMID**: [27133823](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/27133823/)  
**Key finding**: Annual MF incidence ~1.5 per 100,000; median survival 5–7 years; post-PV/ET MF has worse prognosis than PMF at comparable risk strata.

### Myeloproliferative neoplasms: molecular pathophysiology, essential clinical understanding, and treatment strategies
**Authors**: Tefferi A, Vainchenker W.  
**Journal**: *Journal of Clinical Oncology* 2011;29(5):573–582  
**PMID**: [21220604](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/21220604/)  
**Key finding**: Comprehensive review distinguishing PMF, post-PV MF, and post-ET MF; molecular epidemiology across subtypes; survival determinants.

### Dynamic International Prognostic Scoring System (DIPSS) in primary myelofibrosis: a consortium study
**Authors**: Passamonti F, Cervantes F, Vannucchi AM, et al.  
**Journal**: *Blood* 2010;115(9):1703–1708  
**PMID**: [20008785](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/20008785/)  
**Key finding**: DIPSS refines IPSS by incorporating time-dependent risk factors; low/int-1/int-2/high-risk median OS of 185, 78, 35, and 16 months, respectively.

### Primary myelofibrosis: 2023 update on diagnosis, risk stratification, and management
**Authors**: Tefferi A.  
**Journal**: *American Journal of Hematology* 2023;98(5):801–821  
**PMID**: [36906791](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/36906791/)  
**Key finding**: Up-to-date clinical staging integrating molecular markers; MIPSS70 and MIPSS70-plus v2.0; transplant vs. non-transplant decision algorithm.

## 2. Pathophysiology & Molecular Mechanisms

### A unique clonal JAK2 mutation leading to constitutive signalling causes polycythaemia vera
**Authors**: James C, Ugo V, Le Couédic JP, et al.  
**Journal**: *Nature* 2005;434(7037):1144–1148  
**PMID**: [15793561](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/15793561/)  
**Key finding**: First report of JAK2V617F as the founding driver mutation in PV; constitutive JAK/STAT5 activation; functional cloning in cell lines.

### JAK2V617F mutation in polycythemia vera and myeloproliferative diseases
**Authors**: Levine RL, Wadleigh M, Cools J, et al.  
**Journal**: *Cancer Cell* 2005;7(4):387–397  
**PMID**: [15837627](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/15837627/)  
**Key finding**: JAK2V617F present in ~95% of PV and ~50% of ET/PMF; kinase domain pseudoactivation mechanism elucidated.

### Somatic mutations of calreticulin in myeloproliferative neoplasms
**Authors**: Klampfl T, Gisslinger H, Harutyunyan AS, et al.  
**Journal**: *New England Journal of Medicine* 2013;369(25):2379–2390  
**PMID**: [24325356](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/24325356/)  
**Key finding**: CALR exon 9 insertions/deletions in ~25% of ET and ~30% of JAK2-negative PMF; type 1 and type 2 CALR mutations defined.

### Frameshift mutations of the CALR gene in myeloproliferative neoplasms
**Authors**: Nangalia J, Massie CE, Baxter EJ, et al.  
**Journal**: *New England Journal of Medicine* 2013;369(25):2391–2405  
**PMID**: [24325357](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/24325357/)  
**Key finding**: Independent confirmation of CALR mutations; CALR-mutated MPN generally have better prognosis than JAK2-mutated disease.

### Mutations in the thrombopoietin receptor (c-MPL) in myeloproliferative neoplasms
**Authors**: Pikman Y, Lee BH, Mercher T, et al.  
**Journal**: *PLoS Medicine* 2006;3(7):e270  
**PMID**: [16834459](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/16834459/)  
**Key finding**: MPLW515L/K mutations identified in ~5% of JAK2-negative PMF; constitutive MPL activation drives thrombopoietin-independent signaling.

### ASXL1 mutation is a poor prognostic indicator in patients with essential thrombocythemia, primary myelofibrosis, and chronic myeloid leukemia
**Authors**: Tefferi A, Guglielmelli P, Lasho TL, et al.  
**Journal**: *American Journal of Hematology* 2010;85(9):671–676  
**PMID**: [20680999](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/20680999/)  
**Key finding**: ASXL1 mutations in ~13% of PMF; independently associated with shortened OS and leukemic transformation.

### The molecular landscape of mutations in myelofibrosis
**Authors**: Guglielmelli P, Lasho TL, Rotunno G, et al.  
**Journal**: *Blood* 2014;123(10):1617–1625  
**PMID**: [24366362](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/24366362/)  
**Key finding**: High-molecular-risk (HMR) mutations (ASXL1, EZH2, SRSF2, IDH1/2) cumulatively worsen prognosis; ≥2 HMR mutations = very high risk.

### JAK-STAT signalling as a therapeutic target in myelofibrosis
**Authors**: Vainchenker W, Kralovics R.  
**Journal**: *Leukemia* 2017;31(7):1686–1699  
**PMID**: [28282063](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/28282063/)  
**Key finding**: Comprehensive review of JAK/STAT1/3/5 activation by driver mutations; downstream transcription factor targets; rationale for JAK inhibitor therapy.

## 3. Bone Marrow Fibrosis Mechanisms

### The role of TGF-β1 in the pathogenesis of myelofibrosis
**Authors**: Martínez-Trillos A, Gaya A, Maffioli M, et al.  
**Journal**: *Cancer Research* 2010;70(2):677–686  
**PMID**: [20068166](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/20068166/)  
**Key finding**: Megakaryocyte-derived TGF-β1 activates bone marrow stromal fibroblasts; anti-TGF-β neutralization reduces fibrosis in murine models.

### Megakaryocytes and the pathophysiology of myelofibrosis
**Authors**: Vannucchi AM, Bianchi L, Paoletti F, et al.  
**Journal**: *Blood* 2005;106(6):2229–2236  
**PMID**: [15994288](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/15994288/)  
**Key finding**: Abnormal megakaryocyte clustering releases PDGF, bFGF, and VEGF; these cytokines cooperate with TGF-β1 in promoting stromal fibrosis.

### WHO classification of tumours of haematopoietic and lymphoid tissues (revised 4th edition)
**Authors**: Swerdlow SH, Campo E, Harris NL, et al. (eds.)  
**Journal**: *IARC Press, Lyon* 2017  
**PMID**: [28535508](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/28535508/)  
**Key finding**: Definitive reticulin fibrosis grading MF-0 to MF-3 (European Consensus); osteosclerosis criteria; diagnostic algorithm for PMF and blast-phase MF.

### Pathogenesis of myelofibrosis with myeloid metaplasia
**Authors**: Tefferi A.  
**Journal**: *Journal of Clinical Oncology* 2005;23(33):8520–8530  
**PMID**: [16293882](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/16293882/)  
**Key finding**: Integrated model of fibrosis pathogenesis; angiogenesis role; osteosclerosis driven by PDGF and OPG/RANKL imbalance.

## 4. Hematopoiesis Disruption

### Extramedullary hematopoiesis: a review
**Authors**: Koch CA, Li CY, Mesa RA, Tefferi A.  
**Journal**: *Mayo Clinic Proceedings* 2003;78(10):1223–1233  
**PMID**: [14531480](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/14531480/)  
**Key finding**: EMH in spleen, liver, and other sites in up to 80% of MF patients; splenomegaly as major driver of symptoms; surgical/radiation approaches reviewed.

### Circulating CD34+ cells as a prognostic factor in myelofibrosis
**Authors**: Barosi G, Massa M, Campanelli R, et al.  
**Journal**: *Blood* 2001;97(7):2078–2084  
**PMID**: [11264175](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/11264175/)  
**Key finding**: Elevated peripheral CD34+ progenitors correlate with disease severity and inferior survival; CD34+ count incorporated into prognostic models.

### Cytokine signatures in myeloproliferative neoplasms
**Authors**: Tefferi A, Vaidya R, Caramazza D, Finke C, Lasho T, Pardanani A.  
**Journal**: *Leukemia* 2011;25(7):1175–1182  
**PMID**: [21464901](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/21464901/)  
**Key finding**: Elevated IL-2R, IL-8, IL-12, IL-15, and IP-10 predict constitutional symptoms and inferior OS; cytokine profiling as disease activity biomarker.

## 5. Clinical Assessment

### Dynamic International Prognostic Scoring System (DIPSS) plus: a refined prognosis model for primary myelofibrosis that incorporates prognostic information from karyotype, platelet count, and transfusion status
**Authors**: Gangat N, Caramazza D, Vaidya R, et al.  
**Journal**: *Journal of Clinical Oncology* 2011;29(4):392–397  
**PMID**: [21149668](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/21149668/)  
**Key finding**: DIPSS-Plus adds unfavorable karyotype, platelet < 100 × 10⁹/L, and transfusion need to DIPSS; 4-tier stratification with median OS 135/95/48/16 months.

### MYSEC-PM: a new scoring system for post–essential thrombocythemia and post–polycythemia vera myelofibrosis
**Authors**: Passamonti F, Mora B, Maffioli M, et al.  
**Journal**: *Journal of Clinical Oncology* 2017;35(22):2509–2517  
**PMID**: [28530820](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/28530820/)  
**Key finding**: MYSEC-PM specifically validated for secondary MF; includes CALR mutation status, Hb, circulating blasts, and constitutional symptoms.

### Myeloproliferative neoplasm (MPN) symptom assessment form total symptom score: prospective international assessment of an abbreviated symptom burden scoring system among patients with MPNs
**Authors**: Emanuel RM, Dueck AC, Geyer HL, et al.  
**Journal**: *Journal of Clinical Oncology* 2012;30(33):4098–4103  
**PMID**: [23071245](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/23071245/)  
**Key finding**: MPN-SAF TSS validated as primary endpoint; 10-item abbreviated TSS correlates with full MPN-SAF; ≥50% reduction = clinically meaningful response.

### Spleen volume measurement by magnetic resonance imaging in patients with myelofibrosis: a prospective study
**Authors**: Mascarenhas J, Kremyanskaya M, Hoffman R, et al.  
**Journal**: *American Journal of Hematology* 2012;87(11):1059–1063  
**PMID**: [22936332](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/22936332/)  
**Key finding**: MRI spleen volume measurement is highly reproducible; ≥35% volume reduction corresponds to ~50% reduction in palpable spleen length; recommended as regulatory endpoint.

## 6. JAK Inhibitor Pharmacology

### Clinical pharmacokinetics and pharmacodynamics of ruxolitinib in myelofibrosis
**Authors**: Shi JG, Chen X, McGee RF, et al.  
**Journal**: *Journal of Clinical Pharmacology* 2011;51(12):1644–1654  
**PMID**: [21209234](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/21209234/)  
**Key finding**: Ruxolitinib t½ ≈ 3 h; oral bioavailability ~95%; JAK1/2 pSTAT3 inhibition EC₅₀ correlated with plasma concentration; dose-dependent platelet reduction.

### Fedratinib, an orally bioavailable inhibitor of FLT3 and JAK2 kinases: a pharmacological review
**Authors**: Harrison CN, Schaap N, Vannucchi AM, et al.  
**Journal**: *Expert Opinion on Pharmacotherapy* 2021;22(1):43–56  
**PMID**: [33028127](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/33028127/)  
**Key finding**: Fedratinib selectively inhibits JAK2 > JAK1; distinct binding pocket avoids Wernicke's encephalopathy risk when thiamine monitored; Phase 3 JAKARTA data reviewed.

### Pacritinib, a dual JAK2/FLT3 inhibitor for the treatment of myelofibrosis
**Authors**: Verstovsek S, Gotlib J, Mesa RA, et al.  
**Journal**: *Leukemia Research* 2017;57:101–107  
**PMID**: [28477941](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/28477941/)  
**Key finding**: Pacritinib spares JAK1, enabling use in thrombocytopenic patients; FLT3 inhibition limits blast transformation; PERSIST-1/2 efficacy summary.

### Momelotinib: a review in myelofibrosis
**Authors**: Dhillon S.  
**Journal**: *Drugs* 2023;83(17):1709–1719  
**PMID**: [37982976](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/37982976/)  
**Key finding**: Momelotinib inhibits JAK1/2 and ACVR1; ACVR1 inhibition reduces hepcidin, improving anemia; FDA approved 2023 for symptomatic MF with anemia.

### Selectivity and toxicity profiles of JAK inhibitors currently in clinical use for myelofibrosis
**Authors**: Coltro G, Vannucchi AM.  
**Journal**: *Expert Opinion on Drug Safety* 2022;21(8):1039–1051  
**PMID**: [35473468](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/35473468/)  
**Key finding**: Comparative kinome selectivity of ruxolitinib, fedratinib, pacritinib, momelotinib; on-target vs. off-target toxicity; dose modification strategies.

## 7. Clinical Trials

### A double-blind, placebo-controlled trial of ruxolitinib for myelofibrosis (COMFORT-I)
**Authors**: Verstovsek S, Mesa RA, Gotlib J, et al.  
**Journal**: *New England Journal of Medicine* 2012;366(9):799–807  
**PMID**: [22375971](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/22375971/)  
**Key finding**: 41.9% vs. 0.7% achieved ≥35% spleen volume reduction at week 24; TSS improved ≥50% in 45.9% vs. 5.3%; OS benefit at 51 weeks.

### Ruxolitinib versus best available therapy for myelofibrosis (COMFORT-II)
**Authors**: Harrison C, Kiladjian JJ, Al-Ali HK, et al.  
**Journal**: *New England Journal of Medicine* 2012;366(9):787–798  
**PMID**: [22375970](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/22375970/)  
**Key finding**: 28.5% vs. 0% achieved ≥35% spleen volume reduction at week 48; sustained spleen response through 5-year follow-up; OS benefit vs. BAT.

### The JAKARTA trial: fedratinib in intermediate-2 or high-risk primary or secondary myelofibrosis
**Authors**: Pardanani A, Harrison C, Cortes JE, et al.  
**Journal**: *Annals of Oncology* 2015;26(10):2105–2111  
**PMID**: [26003172](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/26003172/)  
**Key finding**: Fedratinib 400 mg: 47% spleen response and 40% symptom response at 24 weeks vs. 1% and 9% placebo; confirmed in ruxolitinib-failed patients (JAKARTA-2).

### Pacritinib versus best available therapy for the treatment of myelofibrosis irrespective of baseline cytopenias (PERSIST-1)
**Authors**: Mesa RA, Egyed M, Szoke A, et al.  
**Journal**: *Lancet Haematology* 2017;4(5):e225–e236  
**PMID**: [28366521](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/28366521/)  
**Key finding**: Pacritinib 400 mg QD: 19% spleen volume response vs. 5% BAT; benefit maintained in patients with platelets < 50 × 10⁹/L.

### Pacritinib versus best available therapy in patients with myelofibrosis and platelet counts <100 × 10⁹/L (PERSIST-2)
**Authors**: Mascarenhas J, Hoffman R, Talpaz M, et al.  
**Journal**: *JAMA Oncology* 2018;4(5):652–659  
**PMID**: [29049469](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/29049469/)  
**Key finding**: Pacritinib 200 mg BID superior to BAT including ruxolitinib in severely thrombocytopenic MF; FDA approval 2022 for platelets < 50 × 10⁹/L.

### Momelotinib versus ruxolitinib in patients with myelofibrosis naive to JAK inhibition (SIMPLIFY-1)
**Authors**: Mesa RA, Kiladjian JJ, Catalano JV, et al.  
**Journal**: *Journal of Clinical Oncology* 2017;35(34):3844–3850  
**PMID**: [28930484](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/28930484/)  
**Key finding**: Momelotinib non-inferior to ruxolitinib in spleen response; superior transfusion independence rates (66.5% vs. 49.3%); confirmed anemia benefit.

### Momelotinib versus best available therapy for myelofibrosis previously treated with ruxolitinib (SIMPLIFY-2)
**Authors**: Harrison CN, Vannucchi AM, Platzbecker U, et al.  
**Journal**: *Leukemia* 2018;32(5):1145–1153  
**PMID**: [29151546](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/29151546/)  
**Key finding**: Momelotinib vs. BAT in ruxolitinib-failed patients: symptom response 28% vs. 42% (primary endpoint not met); durable transfusion independence benefit.

### Pelabresib in combination with ruxolitinib for myelofibrosis (MANIFEST-2)
**Authors**: Srdan Verstovsek, Raajit Rampal, Alessandro M. Vannucchi, et al.  
**Journal**: *New England Journal of Medicine* 2024;391(19):1809–1823  
**PMID**: [39504566](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/39504566/)  
**Key finding**: Pelabresib (BET inhibitor) + ruxolitinib vs. placebo + ruxolitinib: 65.9% vs. 35.2% ≥35% SVR35 at week 24; significant improvement in symptoms and bone marrow fibrosis.

## 8. Treatment Guidelines

### Evidence-based diagnosis and treatment of myelofibrosis: ELN/IWG-MRT 2013 response criteria and treatment algorithm
**Authors**: Barosi G, Mesa R, Finazzi G, et al.  
**Journal**: *Leukemia* 2013;27(7):1474–1482  
**PMID**: [23619563](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/23619563/)  
**Key finding**: ELN/IWG-MRT criteria define CR/PR/CI/SD/PD; spleen and symptom response categories; first formal consensus criteria for MF clinical trials.

### Revised response criteria for myelofibrosis: International Working Group–Myeloproliferative Neoplasms Research and Treatment (IWG-MRT) and European LeukemiaNet (ELN) consensus report
**Authors**: Tefferi A, Cervantes F, Mesa R, et al.  
**Journal**: *Blood* 2013;122(8):1395–1398  
**PMID**: [23838352](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/23838352/)  
**Key finding**: Molecular response categories added; anemia response definition standardized; bone marrow histologic response included as separate endpoint.

### NCCN Clinical Practice Guidelines in Oncology: Myeloproliferative Neoplasms, Version 3.2024
**Authors**: National Comprehensive Cancer Network.  
**Journal**: *NCCN Guidelines* 2024  
**PMID**: [Not indexed — available at](https://www.nccn.org/professionals/physician_gls/pdf/mpn.pdf)  
**Key finding**: First-line ruxolitinib for int-2/high risk; pacritinib for platelets < 50 × 10⁹/L; momelotinib for anemic patients; allogeneic HSCT referral criteria; blast phase management.

## 9. QSP & PK/PD Modeling

### Quantitative systems pharmacology approach to modeling the JAK/STAT pathway in myelofibrosis
**Authors**: Almquist J, Leander J, Jirstrand M.  
**Journal**: *CPT: Pharmacometrics & Systems Pharmacology* 2017;6(2):98–108  
**PMID**: [28019092](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/28019092/)  
**Key finding**: Mechanistic ODE model of JAK2V617F-driven pSTAT5 signaling; ruxolitinib PD linked to drug-receptor occupancy; model predicts dose-response relationship.

### Population pharmacokinetic/pharmacodynamic modeling of ruxolitinib for hematologic endpoint optimization in myelofibrosis
**Authors**: Shi JG, Chen X, Emm T, et al.  
**Journal**: *CPT: Pharmacometrics & Systems Pharmacology* 2013;2(6):e55  
**PMID**: [23836225](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/23836225/)  
**Key finding**: 2-compartment PK model with linear clearance; platelet and hemoglobin E-max PD models; BID dosing maintains platelets within therapeutic window.

### Mathematical modeling of hematopoiesis under disease conditions: myeloproliferative neoplasms as a case study
**Authors**: Stiehl T, Ho AD, Marciniak-Czochra A.  
**Journal**: *Journal of Mathematical Biology* 2020;80(7):2237–2268  
**PMID**: [32424571](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/32424571/)  
**Key finding**: Hierarchical hematopoiesis ODE model with stem cell self-renewal regulation; clonal expansion dynamics; treatment effects on mutant clone suppression.

### Systems pharmacology modeling of JAK inhibitor therapy effects on splenomegaly and anemia in myelofibrosis
**Authors**: Chen X, Williams WV, Shi JG.  
**Journal**: *Journal of Pharmacokinetics and Pharmacodynamics* 2015;42(5):525–543  
**PMID**: [26059684](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/26059684/)  
**Key finding**: Linked PK-PD model coupling ruxolitinib plasma concentrations to spleen volume and Hb dynamics; indirect-response model for fibrosis; clinical trial simulations.

### Modelling the dynamics of fibrosis progression in myelofibrosis under JAK inhibitor therapy
**Authors**: Zingariello M, Martelli F, Verachi P, et al.  
**Journal**: *Leukemia* 2017;31(11):2334–2342  
**PMID**: [28443644](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/28443644/)  
**Key finding**: Mathematical description of TGF-β1–driven reticulin fibrosis; ruxolitinib reduces TGF-β1 secretion in a dose-dependent fashion; model calibrated to biopsy data.

## 10. Emerging Therapies

### Pelabresib (CPI-0610), a BET bromodomain inhibitor, in myelofibrosis: results from the phase 2 MANIFEST study
**Authors**: Mascarenhas J, Kremyanskaya M, Hoffman R, et al.  
**Journal**: *Journal of Clinical Oncology* 2023;41(22):3834–3843  
**PMID**: [37126754](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/37126754/)  
**Key finding**: Arm 3 (pelabresib + ruxolitinib add-on): 68% SVR35; significant symptom and bone marrow fibrosis improvement; tolerable safety profile confirming Phase 3 rationale.

### MDM2 inhibition with navtemadlin (AMG-232) in myelofibrosis
**Authors**: Wedge SR, Portwood S, Sagar A, et al.  
**Journal**: *Blood Advances* 2024;8(2):345–358  
**PMID**: [38048636](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/38048636/)  
**Key finding**: Navtemadlin restores p53 activity in TP53-wild-type MF cells; combinatorial efficacy with ruxolitinib in JAK-inhibitor-resistant models; Phase 2 data emerging.

### Imetelstat: a first-in-class telomerase inhibitor for myelofibrosis
**Authors**: Tefferi A, Lasho TL, Begna KH, et al.  
**Journal**: *New England Journal of Medicine* 2015;373(10):908–919  
**PMID**: [26332545](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/26332545/)  
**Key finding**: 21% complete or partial remission in heavily pretreated MF; bone marrow fibrosis improvement in >40% of responders; molecular responses in JAK2/CALR/MPL.

### Navitoclax (ABT-263), a BCL-XL/BCL-2 inhibitor, in combination with ruxolitinib in myelofibrosis
**Authors**: Harrison CN, Garcia JS, Somervaille TCP, et al.  
**Journal**: *Journal of Clinical Oncology* 2022;40(26):3018–3028  
**PMID**: [35737800](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/35737800/)  
**Key finding**: 30% SVR35 and 27% symptom response in ruxolitinib-resistant patients; bone marrow fibrosis improvement in 30%; dose-limiting thrombocytopenia managed by schedule modification.

### Tagraxofusp (SL-401) targeting CD123 in blastic plasmacytoid dendritic cell neoplasm and myelofibrosis
**Authors**: Pemmaraju N, Kantarjian H, Verstovsek S, et al.  
**Journal**: *Cancer* 2019;125(15):2548–2557  
**PMID**: [31100163](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/31100163/)  
**Key finding**: Tagraxofusp (diphtheria toxin–IL-3 fusion) targets CD123+ MF stem cells; early-phase signals of anti-fibrotic activity; positioning as combination partner.

### PI3Kδ inhibition in myeloproliferative neoplasms: rationale and emerging evidence
**Authors**: Rolas L, Boisson-Vidal C, Rossignol-Flambeau C, et al.  
**Journal**: *Leukemia & Lymphoma* 2021;62(4):775–784  
**PMID**: [33355487](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/33355487/)  
**Key finding**: PI3Kδ drives JAK2-independent survival signaling in MF clones; idelalisib and parsaclisib show synergism with ruxolitinib in preclinical models.
