## 1. Pathophysiology & Disease Mechanisms (10 refs)

1. **Kahn SE, Cooper ME, Del Prato S.** (2014). Pathophysiology and treatment of type 2 diabetes: perspectives on the past, present, and future. *Lancet*, 383(9922):1068–1083.  
   https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/24315620/  
   *Key review on insulin resistance, β-cell failure, and the dual defects of T2DM.*

2. **DeFronzo RA.** (2009). Banting Lecture. From the triumvirate to the ominous octet: a new paradigm for the treatment of type 2 diabetes mellitus. *Diabetes*, 58(4):773–795.  
   https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/19336687/  
   *Classic paper identifying 8 pathophysiological defects in T2DM.*

3. **Roden M, Shulman GI.** (2019). The integrative biology of type 2 diabetes. *Nature*, 576(7785):51–60.  
   https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/31802013/  
   *Systems-level view of T2DM pathophysiology including mitochondria, epigenetics, and multi-organ interplay.*

4. **Butler AE, Janson J, Bonner-Weir S, Ritzel R, Rizza RA, Butler PC.** (2003). β-cell deficit and increased β-cell apoptosis in humans with type 2 diabetes. *Diabetes*, 52(1):102–110.  
   https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/12502499/  
   *Quantifies ~60% reduction in β-cell mass in T2DM; basis for β-cell mass ODE.*

5. **Topp B, Promislow K, deVries G, Miura RM, Finegood DT.** (2000). A model of β-cell mass, insulin, and glucose kinetics: pathways to diabetes. *J Theor Biol*, 206(4):605–619.  
   https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/11013117/  
   *Mathematical model of β-cell mass dynamics — direct basis for β-cell proliferation/apoptosis ODEs.*

6. **Weir GC, Bonner-Weir S.** (2004). Five stages of evolving beta-cell dysfunction during progression to diabetes. *Diabetes*, 53(Suppl 3):S16–S21.  
   https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/15561905/  
   *Stage progression model of β-cell dysfunction.*

7. **Stumvoll M, Goldstein BJ, van Haeften TW.** (2005). Type 2 diabetes: principles of pathogenesis and therapy. *Lancet*, 365(9467):1333–1346.  
   https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/15823385/

8. **Samuel VT, Shulman GI.** (2012). Mechanisms for insulin resistance: common threads and missing links. *Cell*, 148(5):852–871.  
   https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/22385956/  
   *DAG-PKCθ pathway, ceramide, and IRS-1 serine phosphorylation in insulin resistance.*

9. **Hotamisligil GS.** (2017). Inflammation, metaflammation and immunometabolic disorders. *Nature*, 542(7640):177–185.  
   https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/28179656/  
   *Chronic low-grade inflammation (metaflammation) linking obesity to T2DM.*

10. **Donnelly KL, Smith CI, Schwarzenberg SJ, Jessurun J, Boldt MD, Parks EJ.** (2005). Sources of fatty acids stored in liver and secreted via lipoproteins in patients with nonalcoholic fatty liver disease. *J Clin Invest*, 115(5):1343–1351.  
    https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/15864352/  
    *FFA contribution to hepatic lipid accumulation and NAFLD in T2DM.*
