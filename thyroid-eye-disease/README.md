# Thyroid Eye Disease (TED / Graves' Orbitopathy, 갑상선眼병증) — QSP Model

> Integrated Quantitative Systems Pharmacology model of TED, linking the
> systemic TSHR-TRAb autoimmune axis of Graves' disease to its orbital
> cross-reaction with fibroblast IGF-1R — the key mechanistic node that
> drives T/B-cell infiltration, cytokine-mediated orbital fibroblast
> activation, hyaluronan/GAG-driven edema, adipogenesis and late-phase
> fibrosis — culminating in proptosis, eyelid retraction, diplopia and
> (rarely) compressive dysthyroid optic neuropathy (DON). The model spans
> the modern pharmacology stack (teprotumumab, IV methylprednisolone/oral
> prednisone, rituximab, tocilizumab, selenium) together with smoking as a
> key modifiable risk factor and Rundle's-curve natural history.

## 1. Disease in one paragraph

TED (Graves' orbitopathy) develops in ~25-50% of patients with Graves'
disease when TSHR-stimulating autoantibodies (TRAb/TSI), generated by
loss of tolerance to the thyroid TSH receptor, cross-react with a
TSHR-IGF-1R signaling complex expressed on orbital fibroblasts (Bahn 2010
NEJM). This crosstalk, amplified by infiltrating Th1/Th17 T cells, B
cells and macrophages secreting IL-1β/IL-6/TNF-α/IFN-γ, activates orbital
fibroblasts to differentiate into adipocytes (orbital fat expansion) or
myofibroblasts (TGF-β-driven fibrosis) and to overproduce hyaluronan,
whose osmotic water-binding causes extraocular muscle (EOM) edema and
conjunctival chemosis. Because the bony orbit cannot expand, increased
fat/muscle volume raises intraorbital pressure, producing proptosis
(Hertel exophthalmometry), eyelid retraction (sympathetic Müller-muscle
overactivity), restrictive diplopia (fibrotic EOM tethering) and — when
apex crowding compresses the optic nerve — sight-threatening dysthyroid
optic neuropathy (DON). Disease activity is graded by the Clinical
Activity Score (CAS 0-7, Mourits 1997) and EUGOGO severity class; the
natural history typically follows Rundle's curve — an active
inflammatory phase (6-24 months) that gradually transitions to a
quiescent fibrotic phase in which inflammation resolves but structural
change (fibrosis, fixed proptosis/diplopia) may persist. Smoking is the
single strongest modifiable risk factor, dose-dependently worsening
severity and blunting response to glucocorticoids and radiotherapy.
First-line therapy for active moderate-severe disease is IV
methylprednisolone or, since 2020, the IGF-1R-blocking antibody
teprotumumab (OPTIC trial, 83% proptosis responders vs 10% placebo);
rituximab (anti-CD20) and tocilizumab (anti-IL-6R) are used in
steroid-resistant disease with more heterogeneous trial evidence, and
selenium is EUGOGO-endorsed for mild disease.

## 2. Mechanistic clusters (12 in the DOT map, 104 nodes)

1. Systemic autoimmunity — TSHR-TRAb axis (Graves' disease, RAI/thyroidectomy, ATD)
2. Orbital immune cell infiltration (Th1/Th17, Treg deficiency, B cells, macrophages, cytokines)
3. TSHR-IGF-1R crosstalk on orbital fibroblasts (the key teprotumumab target node)
4. Orbital fibroblast fate decision (adipogenic PPAR-γ vs myofibroblast/TGF-β-CTGF)
5. Glycosaminoglycan/hyaluronan & ECM dynamics
6. Orbital tissue remodeling (fat volume, EOM volume/edema, fibrosis)
7. Mechanical/compressive consequences (proptosis, apex crowding, DON, corneal exposure)
8. Clinical activity, severity & PRO endpoints (CAS, EUGOGO class, GO-QoL, NOSPECS)
9. Drug PK/PD — teprotumumab (IGF-1R mAb)
10. Drug PK/PD — glucocorticoid, rituximab, tocilizumab, selenium
11. Non-pharmacologic/procedural therapy (smoking cessation, orbital RT/decompression, strabismus/lid surgery)
12. Adverse effects & safety monitoring (hearing loss, hyperglycemia, Cushingoid, hepatotoxicity)

## 3. mrgsolve model (20 ODE compartments)

* **Drug PK (4 agents, 8 compartments)** — teprotumumab (2-cmpt IgG),
  glucocorticoid genomic-signal compartment (IV methylprednisolone bolus
  or oral prednisone with absorption), rituximab (2-cmpt IgG), tocilizumab
  (2-cmpt, simplified from TMDD).
* **Orbital autoimmune/fibroblast disease network (10 compartments)** —
  B-cell pool, TRAb titer, T-cell activation, cytokine drive, fibroblast
  activation, hyaluronan/GAG index, orbital fat volume, EOM volume/edema,
  fibrosis index, Clinical Activity Score.
* **Safety trackers (2 compartments)** — cumulative teprotumumab AUC
  (hearing-impairment risk) and cumulative glucocorticoid dose
  (Cushingoid/hepatotoxicity risk).

## 4. Shiny dashboard (8 tabs)

1. **Patient profile** — adjustable age/weight/smoking/baseline CAS/severity/duration.
2. **Drug PK** — plasma concentrations of the three tracked mAbs + glucocorticoid signal.
3. **Immune / Fibroblast PD** — receptor occupancy, B-cell/TRAb, T-cell/cytokine, fibroblast/hyaluronan.
4. **Orbital tissue remodeling** — orbital fat volume, EOM volume/edema, fibrosis index.
5. **Clinical endpoints** — CAS, Hertel proptosis, GO-QoL, EUGOGO active-disease flag.
6. **Scenario comparison** — runs all 9 scenarios with the chosen profile.
7. **Biomarkers & safety** — teprotumumab AUC/hearing-AE risk, cumulative glucocorticoid/Cushingoid-AE risk.
8. **References** — key citations and link to the full bibliography.

## 5. How to run

```bash
# 1) Render the mechanistic map
dot -Tsvg ted_qsp_model.dot -o ted_qsp_model.svg
dot -Tpng -Gdpi=150 ted_qsp_model.dot -o ted_qsp_model.png
```

```r
# 2) Simulate scenarios in R
install.packages(c("mrgsolve","dplyr","tidyr","ggplot2","shiny",
                   "shinydashboard","DT"))
library(mrgsolve)
mod <- mread_cache("ted_mrgsolve_model.R")
out <- mod %>% ev(amt=10*70, cmt="CEN_TEP", time=0) %>%
  ev_seq(ev(amt=20*70, cmt="CEN_TEP", ii=21*24, addl=6, time=21*24)) %>%
  mrgsim(end=17520, delta=24)
plot(out, c("Proptosis_mm","CAS_score","IGF1R_occupancy","GOQOL_score"))

# 3) Launch the dashboard
shiny::runApp("ted_shiny_app.R")
```

## 6. Key clinical anchors used during calibration

| Endpoint | Comparator | Expected effect |
|---|---|---|
| Proptosis responder (≥2mm ↓) | Teprotumumab vs placebo, wk24 | 83% vs 10% (Douglas 2020 NEJM, OPTIC) |

## 7. Caveats

* Designed for **research, education, and hypothesis generation** — not
  clinical decision support.
* All PK/PD parameters are population means or literature-informed
  surrogates; inter-individual variability needs `omega()` blocks for
  population simulations.
* Thyroid hormone/TSH dynamics themselves are not modeled explicitly
  (TRAb is tracked as an orbital-disease driver, not a thyroid-function
  simulator) — pair with a dedicated Graves'-disease thyroid-axis model
  for combined thyrotoxicosis + orbitopathy simulation.
* The glucocorticoid compartment represents genomic anti-inflammatory
  persistence (not true plasma steroid pharmacokinetics); cumulative-dose
  bookkeeping (`CUMGC`) must be incremented via a matching dosing event
  in R alongside each `GC_C`/`GUT_GC` dose (see usage notes in the model
  file).
* Diplopia and dysthyroid optic neuropathy are represented qualitatively
  via the EOM-volume/fibrosis and apex-crowding nodes in the mechanistic
  map rather than as separate quantitative ODE endpoints.
