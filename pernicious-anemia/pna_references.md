## 1. Epidemiology & Disease Overview (역학 및 질환 개요)

1. **Lahner E & Annibale B (2009)**. Pernicious anemia: new insights from a gastroenterological point of view. *World J Gastroenterol*, 15(41):5121–8.  
   PMID: [19860998](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/19860998/)  
   *Key data*: PA prevalence ~0.1% general population; female predominance; median age 60; association with other autoimmune disorders in 30% of cases.

2. **Stabler SP (2013)**. Vitamin B12 deficiency. *N Engl J Med*, 368(2):149–60.  
   PMID: [23301732](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/23301732/)  
   *Key data*: Comprehensive clinical review of B12 deficiency etiology, manifestations, and treatment; diagnostic algorithm for MMA and tHcy elevation.

3. **Oh R & Brown DL (2003)**. Vitamin B12 deficiency. *Am Fam Physician*, 67(5):979–86.  
   PMID: [12643357](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/12643357/)  
   *Key data*: B12 deficiency prevalence ~6% in adults >60; PA accounts for 15–25% of deficiency cases in older adults.

4. **Toh BH, van Driel IR & Gleeson PA (1997)**. Pernicious anemia. *N Engl J Med*, 337(20):1441–8.  
   PMID: [9358143](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/9358143/)  
   *Key data*: Seminal review of PA pathophysiology; autoimmune mechanism, genetic predisposition (HLA-DR3/DR4), and association with type 1 diabetes and autoimmune thyroid disease.

5. **Lam-Tse WK et al. (2002)**. Association between autoimmune thyroid disease and pernicious anemia. *Eur J Endocrinol*, 147(5):611–5.  
   PMID: [12444892](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/12444892/)  
   *Key data*: 30% of PA patients have concurrent autoimmune thyroid disease; suggests shared immune pathogenesis.

   *Key data*: Global prevalence of corpus atrophic gastritis (CAG); H. pylori as primary cause; PA as advanced stage of CAG.

9. **Borch K et al. (1997)**. Gastric atrophy: prevalence and relation to other gastrointestinal symptoms and phenotyping. *J Clin Gastroenterol*, 25(1):145–50.

## 3. Cobalamin Absorption Physiology (코발라민 흡수 생리)

    PMID: [10080185](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/10080185/)  
    *Key data*: Molecular characterization of cubilin as the essential receptor for IF-B12 complex at the ileal brush border.

14. **Fedosov SN (2012)**. Physiological and molecular aspects of cobalamin transport. *Subcell Biochem*, 56:347–67.  
    PMID: [22116704](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/22116704/)  
    *Key data*: Plasma transport proteins TC-I, TC-II, TC-III; HoloTC (active B12) kinetics; tissue uptake via CD320 receptor.

15. **Hall CA (1984)**. Transcobalamin I and II as natural transport proteins of vitamin B12. *J Clin Invest*, 56(5):1125–31.  
    PMID: [4215910](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/4215910/)  
    *Key data*: TC-I (~80% of plasma B12 as storage form), TC-II (~20% as active delivery form); enterohepatic recycling kinetics.

    PMID: [31193809](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/31193809/)  
    *Key data*: HoloTC <35 pmol/L as earliest marker of depletion; serum B12 <148 pmol/L (200 pg/mL) is late-stage; MMA and tHcy as functional deficiency markers.

30. **Carmel R (1996)**. Cobalamin, the stomach, and aging. *Am J Clin Nutr*, 64(1):77–83.  
    PMID: [8669409](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/8669409/)  
    *Key data*: Pharmacokinetics of hydroxocobalamin vs. cyanocobalamin; OH-Cbl has higher protein binding (longer half-life); IM depot absorption rate constants.

    *Key data*: Intranasal cyanocobalamin (Nascobal 500 µg/week) effective for maintenance after initial IM treatment; buccal/sublingual routes discussed.
