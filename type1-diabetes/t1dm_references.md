## 1. Disease Overview & Epidemiology

1. **International Diabetes Federation (IDF) Diabetes Atlas, 11th ed. (2023)**
   - Global T1DM incidence ~98,000 new pediatric cases/year; lifetime prevalence ~8.4 million
   - [https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/36812948/](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/36812948/)

2. **Patterson CC, et al. — Trends and cyclical variation in the incidence of childhood type 1 diabetes in 26 European centres in the 25 year period 1989–2013: a multicentre prospective registration study. *Diabetologia* 2019**
   - 3–4% annual incidence increase; Northern Europe highest rates
   - [https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/30868213/](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/30868213/)

3. **Katsarou A, et al. — Type 1 diabetes mellitus. *Nat Rev Dis Primers* 2017**
   - Comprehensive disease overview including pathogenesis, genetics, and treatment
   - [https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/28358037/](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/28358037/)

## 2. Immunopathogenesis

4. **Bluestone JA, et al. — Genetics, pathogenesis and clinical interventions in type 1 diabetes. *Nature* 2010**
   - T cell–mediated beta-cell destruction; CD8+ CTL, Treg dysfunction
   - [https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/20393543/](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/20393543/)

5. **Atkinson MA, Eisenbarth GS, Michels AW — Type 1 diabetes. *Lancet* 2014**
   - Stages 1–3 classification; insulitis and beta-cell destruction mechanisms
   - [https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/23890997/](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/23890997/)

6. **Warram JH, et al. — HLA-DR4 and type 1 diabetes. *N Engl J Med* 1984**
   - Foundational HLA association; DR3/DR4 heterozygosity risk
   - [https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/6327601/](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/6327601/)

7. **Todd JA, et al. — Robust associations of four new chromosome regions from genome-wide analyses of type 1 diabetes. *Nat Genet* 2007**
   - PTPN22, CTLA4, IL2RA, IFIH1 T1DM susceptibility loci
   - [https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/17554260/](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/17554260/)

8. **Imagawa A, et al. — Pancreatic biopsy as a procedure for detecting in situ autoimmune phenomena in type 1 diabetes. *Diabetes* 2001**
   - Insulitis histology; CD8+/CD4+ infiltration characterisation
   - [https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/11473019/](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/11473019/)

9. **Kent SC, et al. — Expanded T cells from pancreatic lymph nodes of type 1 diabetic subjects recognise an insulin epitope. *Nature* 2005**
   - Proinsulin peptide (C-A junction) targeted by CD8+ T cells in T1DM
   - [https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/15793566/](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/15793566/)

10. **Baekkeskov S, et al. — Identification of the 64K autoantigen in insulin-dependent diabetes as the GABA-synthesizing enzyme glutamic acid decarboxylase. *Nature* 1990**
    - Discovery of GAD65 (GAD-2) as major T1DM autoantigen
    - [https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/1970680/](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/1970680/)

11. **Wasserfall C, et al. — Persistence of pancreatic insulin mRNA expression and proinsulin protein in type 1 diabetes pancreata. *Cell Metab* 2017**
    - Residual insulin expression even in longstanding T1DM pancreata
    - [https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/28380375/](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/28380375/)

## 8. Immunotherapy — Teplizumab & Disease Modification

29. **Herold KC, et al. — An anti-CD3 antibody, teplizumab, in relatives at risk for type 1 diabetes. *N Engl J Med* 2019 (TN-10 trial)**
    - Single 14-day course delayed T1DM onset ~2.9 years (p < 0.001); landmark trial
    - [https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/31180800/](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/31180800/)

30. **Sims EK, et al. — Teplizumab improves and stabilizes beta cell function in antibody-positive high-risk individuals. *Sci Transl Med* 2021**
    - C-peptide AUC preserved at 2 years; Treg expansion mechanism
    - [https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/33789958/](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/33789958/)

31. **Herold KC, et al. — Teplizumab (anti-CD3 mAb) treatment preserves C-peptide responses in patients with new-onset type 1 diabetes in a randomized controlled trial: metabolic and immunologic features at baseline identify a subgroup of responders. *Diabetes* 2013**
    - mAb mechanism: TCR internalization + exhausted CTL phenotype
    - [https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/23520283/](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/23520283/)

32. **Orban T, et al. — Co-stimulation modulation with abatacept in patients with recent-onset type 1 diabetes: a randomised, double-blind, placebo-controlled trial. *Lancet* 2011**
    - Abatacept (CTLA4-Ig): C-peptide AUC preserved +59% at 2 years
    - [https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/21620564/](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/21620564/)

33. **Pescovitz MD, et al. — Rituximab, B-lymphocyte depletion, and preservation of beta-cell function. *N Engl J Med* 2009**
    - Anti-CD20 rituximab: HbA1c advantage at 1 year; C-peptide preserved
    - [https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/19940299/](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/19940299/)

34. **Gitelman SE, et al. — Antithymocyte globulin treatment for patients with recent-onset type 1 diabetes: 12-month results of a randomised, placebo-controlled, phase 2 trial. *Lancet Diabetes Endocrinol* 2013**
    - ATG+G-CSF: C-peptide preservation at 12 months; Treg induction
    - [https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/24579979/](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/24579979/)

35. **Todd JA, et al. — Regulatory T cell responses in participants with type 1 diabetes after a single course of daclizumab (anti-CD25). *J Clin Invest* 2007**
    - Anti-CD25: Treg expansion; mixed results on beta-cell preservation
    - [https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/17143333/](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/17143333/)

36. **von Herrath M, et al. — Anti-interleukin-21 antibody and liraglutide for the preservation of β-cell function in adults with recent-onset type 1 diabetes: a randomised, double-blind, placebo-controlled phase 2 trial. *Lancet Diabetes Endocrinol* 2021**
    - Anti-IL-21 + GLP-1RA: C-peptide AUC +42% at 1 year
    - [https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/33864366/](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/33864366/)

## 10. Complications

40. **The Diabetes Control and Complications Trial Research Group — The effect of intensive treatment of diabetes on the development and progression of long-term complications in insulin-dependent diabetes mellitus. *N Engl J Med* 1993 (DCCT)**
    - Intensive glucose control reduces retinopathy 76%, nephropathy 54%, neuropathy 60%
    - [https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/8366922/](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/8366922/)

41. **Nathan DM, et al. — Intensive diabetes treatment and cardiovascular disease in patients with type 1 diabetes (DCCT/EDIC). *N Engl J Med* 2005**
    - 17-year EDIC follow-up: CV event reduction 42%; metabolic memory concept
    - [https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/16360910/](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/16360910/)

42. **Brownlee M — The pathobiology of diabetic complications: a unifying mechanism. *Diabetes* 2005**
    - Mitochondrial superoxide → AGE/RAGE/PKC/polyol/hexosamine pathways
    - [https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/15919781/](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/15919781/)

43. **Nathan DM — Translating the A1C assay into estimated average glucose values. *Diabetes Care* 2008 (ADAG study)**
    - ADAG equation: eAG(mg/dL) = 28.7 × HbA1c − 46.7
    - [https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/18540046/](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/18540046/)
