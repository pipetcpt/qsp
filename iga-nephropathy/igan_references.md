# IgA Nephropathy (IgAN) — QSP Model References

> Curated PubMed literature covering the four-hit pathogenesis, molecular mechanisms,
> biomarkers, clinical trials, and drug targets relevant to the QSP model.

---

## 1. Pathogenesis — Four-Hit Hypothesis

1. **Suzuki H, Kiryluk K, Novak J, et al.** The pathophysiology of IgA nephropathy. *J Am Soc Nephrol*. 2011;22(10):1795-1803.  
   <https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/21949093/>  
   _Seminal review establishing the four-hit model; Gd-IgA1 overproduction, autoantibody, IC formation, mesangial injury._

2. **Novak J, Rizk D, Takahashi K, et al.** New insights into the pathogenesis of IgA nephropathy. *Kidney Dis*. 2015;1(1):8-18.  
   <https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/27340649/>  
   _Detailed mechanistic overview; role of Gd-IgA1, immune complexes, and kidney injury cascade._

3. **Wyatt RJ, Julian BA.** IgA nephropathy. *N Engl J Med*. 2013;368(25):2402-2414.  
   <https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/23782179/>  
   _Comprehensive clinical-mechanistic review; standard reference for pathophysiology and natural history._

4. **Coppo R.** IgA nephropathy: a European perspective in the increasingly global problem of kidney disease. *Kidney Int Suppl*. 2016;6(1):10-14.  
   <https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/30675433/>

5. **Knoppova B, Reily C, Maillard N, et al.** The origin and activities of IgA1-containing immune complexes in IgA nephropathy. *Front Immunol*. 2016;7:117.  
   <https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/27066008/>  
   _Focus on IC size, complement activation, and mesangial deposition mechanisms._

## 2. Hit 1 — Gd-IgA1 Glycobiology

6. **Mestecky J, Raska M, Julian BA, et al.** IgA nephropathy: molecular mechanisms of the disease. *Annu Rev Pathol*. 2013;8:217-240.  
   <https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/23347348/>  
   _In-depth biochemistry of O-glycan deficiency in IgA1 hinge region; C1GalT1/Cosmc axis._

7. **Suzuki H, Suzuki Y, Narita I, et al.** Toll-like receptor 9 affects severity of IgA nephropathy. *J Am Soc Nephrol*. 2008;19(12):2384-2395.  
   <https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/18829464/>  
   _TLR9 signaling in tonsillar B cells drives Gd-IgA1 production; mucosal trigger._

8. **Takahashi K, Wall SB, Suzuki H, et al.** Clustered O-glycans of IgA1: defining macro- and microheterogeneity by use of electron capture/transfer dissociation. *Mol Cell Proteomics*. 2010;9(11):2545-2557.  
   <https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/20601650/>

9. **Kiryluk K, Li Y, Scolari F, et al.** Discovery of new risk loci for IgA nephropathy implicates genes involved in immunity against intestinal pathogens. *Nat Genet*. 2014;46(11):1187-1196.  
   <https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/25305756/>  
   _GWAS findings; TNFSF13 (APRIL), DEFA1A3, HLA loci as major genetic risk factors._

10. **Gharavi AG, Kiryluk K, Choi M, et al.** Genome-wide association study identifies susceptibility loci for IgA nephropathy. *Nat Genet*. 2011;43(4):321-327.  
    <https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/21399633/>

## 3. Hit 2 — Autoantibody Formation

11. **Tomana M, Novak J, Julian BA, et al.** Circulating immune complexes in IgA nephropathy consist of IgA1 with galactose-deficient hinge region and antiglycan antibodies. *J Clin Invest*. 1999;104(1):73-81.  
    <https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/10393701/>  
    _Original demonstration that autoantibodies target GalNAc on Gd-IgA1._

12. **Berthoux F, Suzuki H, Thibaudin L, et al.** Autoantibodies targeting galactose-deficient IgA1 associate with progression of IgA nephropathy. *J Am Soc Nephrol*. 2012;23(9):1579-1587.  
    <https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/22773827/>  
    _Clinical correlation of anti-Gd-IgA1 IgG with disease progression and ESKD._

13. **Suzuki H, Fan R, Zhang Z, et al.** Aberrantly glycosylated IgA1 in IgA nephropathy patients is recognized by IgG antibodies with restricted heterogeneity. *J Clin Invest*. 2009;119(6):1668-1677.  
    <https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/19436114/>  
    _Characterization of anti-GalNAc IgG; somatic hypermutation, restricted BCR usage._

## 12. Clinical Trials — Budesonide (Targeted Release)

33. **Barratt J, Lafayette R, Suzuki Y, et al.** Results from Part B of the NefIgArd trial: a phase 3 randomized trial of targeted-release budesonide (Nefecon) for primary IgA nephropathy. *Kidney Int*. 2023;103(2):391-402.  
    <https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/36243144/>  
    _Phase 3 RCT; budesonide TRF 16mg/d significantly reduced proteinuria (UPCR −22%) and preserved eGFR vs. placebo._

34. **Lv J, Zhang H, Wong MG, et al.** Effect of oral methylprednisolone on clinical outcomes in patients with IgA nephropathy: the TESTING randomized clinical trial. *JAMA*. 2017;317(9):925-934.  
    <https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/28241403/>  
    _TESTING trial; systemic steroids reduce proteinuria but increase serious adverse events._
