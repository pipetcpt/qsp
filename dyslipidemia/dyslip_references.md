## 1. Pathophysiology & Mechanisms

### 1.1 Cholesterol Biosynthesis — HMG-CoA Reductase Pathway

1. [Goldstein JL, Brown MS. 1990. Regulation of the mevalonate pathway. *Nature*. PMID: 1969820](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/1969820/)
   *Landmark review of cholesterol biosynthesis via HMG-CoA reductase and the mevalonate cascade.*

2. [Horton JD, Goldstein JL, Brown MS. 2002. SREBPs: activators of the complete program of cholesterol and fatty acid synthesis in the liver. *J Clin Invest*. PMID: 11827993](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/11827993/)
   *SREBP-mediated transcriptional control of lipogenic genes including HMGCR.*

3. [Goldstein JL, Brown MS. 2015. A century of cholesterol and coronaries: from plaques to genes to statins. *Cell*. PMID: 25748148](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/25748148/)
   *Comprehensive historical review of cholesterol biology and its therapeutic implications.*

### 1.2 LDL Receptor Biology and Regulation

4. [Brown MS, Goldstein JL. 1986. A receptor-mediated pathway for cholesterol homeostasis. *Science*. PMID: 3513311](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/3513311/)
   *Nobel Prize-winning description of the LDL receptor pathway and familial hypercholesterolemia.*

5. [Rader DJ, Cohen J, Hobbs HH. 2003. Monogenic hypercholesterolemia: new insights in pathogenesis and treatment. *J Clin Invest*. PMID: 12727915](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/12727915/)

### 1.3 PCSK9 Discovery and Function

6. [Abifadel M et al. 2003. Mutations in PCSK9 cause autosomal dominant hypercholesterolemia. *Nat Genet*. PMID: 12730697](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/12730697/)
   *Discovery paper identifying gain-of-function PCSK9 mutations as a cause of FH.*

7. [Cohen JC et al. 2006. Sequence variations in PCSK9, low LDL, and protection against coronary heart disease. *N Engl J Med*. PMID: 16554528](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/16554528/)
   *Human genetic evidence that loss-of-function PCSK9 mutations lower LDL and reduce CHD risk.*

8. [Seidah NG et al. 2003. The secretory proprotein convertase neural apoptosis-regulated convertase 1 (NARC-1): liver regeneration and neuronal differentiation. *Proc Natl Acad Sci USA*. PMID: 14576349](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/14576349/)
   *Original characterization of PCSK9 as a secreted proprotein convertase.*

### 1.4 Lipoprotein Metabolism — VLDL Assembly, ApoB, LPL, CETP

9. [Ginsberg HN. 2000. Insulin resistance and cardiovascular disease. *J Clin Invest*. PMID: 10953026](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/10953026/)
   *Role of insulin resistance in VLDL overproduction and hypertriglyceridemia.*

10. [Mahley RW, Innerarity TL, Rall SC Jr, Weisgraber KH. 1984. Plasma lipoproteins: apolipoprotein structure and function. *J Lipid Res*. PMID: 6392221](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/6392221/)

11. [Tall AR. 1993. Plasma cholesteryl ester transfer protein. *J Lipid Res*. PMID: 8245716](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/8245716/)

12. [Wang H, Eckel RH. 2009. Lipoprotein lipase: from gene expression to atherosclerosis. *Am J Physiol Endocrinol Metab*. PMID: 19887600](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/19887600/)
    *LPL-mediated triglyceride hydrolysis and its regulation in metabolic disease.*

### 1.5 Reverse Cholesterol Transport — HDL, ABCA1, SR-BI

13. [Tall AR, Yvan-Charvet L. 2015. Cholesterol, inflammation and innate immunity. *Nat Rev Immunol*. PMID: 25614320](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/25614320/)

14. [Rader DJ, Alexander ET, Weibel GL, Billheimer J, Rothblat GH. 2009. The role of reverse cholesterol transport in animals and humans and relationship to atherosclerosis. *J Lipid Res*. PMID: 18779614](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/18779614/)

### 1.6 Foam Cell Formation and Atherogenesis

### 1.7 Lp(a) Biology

19. [Kamstrup PR, Tybjaerg-Hansen A, Steffensen R, Nordestgaard BG. 2009. Genetically elevated lipoprotein(a) and increased risk of myocardial infarction. *JAMA*. PMID: 19318653](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/19318653/)

## 3. Drug Mechanisms & PK/PD

### 3.1 Statins

25. [Endo A. 1992. The discovery and development of HMG-CoA reductase inhibitors. *J Lipid Res*. PMID: 1464741](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/1464741/)
    *Discoverer's account of the isolation of compactin and the statin drug class.*

26. [Bellosta S, Paoletti R, Corsini A. 2004. Safety of statins: focus on clinical pharmacokinetics and drug interactions. *Circulation*. PMID: 15611362](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/15611362/)
    *Comparative PK of major statins (atorvastatin, rosuvastatin, simvastatin): absorption, CYP450 metabolism, half-life.*

27. [Bergman E et al. 2009. Rosuvastatin pharmacokinetics: an integrated population PK model. *Clin Pharmacol Ther*. PMID: 19536065](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/19536065/)
    *Population PK modeling of rosuvastatin with hepatic uptake (OATP1B1) and biliary excretion.*

### 3.2 PCSK9 Inhibitors

28. [Roth EM et al. 2012. Atorvastatin with or without an antibody inhibiting PCSK9 (evolocumab) in primary hypercholesterolaemia and mixed dyslipidaemia: a multicentre, double-blind, randomised, placebo-controlled trial. *Lancet*. PMID: 22633834](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/22633834/)
    *Phase 2 proof-of-concept for PCSK9 antibody evolocumab achieving >60% LDL-C reduction.*

29. [Dijk W, Cariou B. 2020. Efficacy and safety of proprotein convertase subtilisin/kexin 9 inhibitors in people with diabetes. *Diabetes Obes Metab*. PMID: 32026617](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/32026617/)
    *Mechanistic and clinical review of PCSK9 inhibitor PK/PD and metabolic effects.*

### 3.3 Inclisiran (siRNA)

30. [Fitzgerald K et al. 2017. Effect of an RNA interference drug on the synthesis of proprotein convertase subtilisin/kexin type 9 (PCSK9) and the concentration of serum LDL cholesterol in healthy volunteers. *N Engl J Med*. PMID: 27959715](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/27959715/)
    *First-in-human siRNA targeting PCSK9 with durable LDL-C lowering for up to 180 days.*

### 3.4 Ezetimibe

31. [Altmann SW et al. 2004. Niemann-Pick C1 Like 1 protein is critical for intestinal cholesterol absorption. *Science*. PMID: 15001771](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/15001771/)
    *Identification of NPC1L1 as the intestinal cholesterol transporter inhibited by ezetimibe.*

### 3.5 Bempedoic Acid

32. [Pinkosky SL et al. 2016. Liver-specific ATP-citrate lyase inhibition by bempedoic acid decreases LDL-C and attenuates atherosclerosis. *Nat Commun*. PMID: 27748957](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/27748957/)
    *Mechanism of bempedoic acid: ACL inhibition upstream of HMG-CoA reductase reduces hepatic cholesterol synthesis.*

### 3.6 Fibrates and PPARα

33. [Staels B et al. 1998. Mechanism of action of fibrates on lipid and lipoprotein metabolism. *Circulation*. PMID: 9826316](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/9826326/)
    *PPARα agonism by fibrates enhances fatty acid oxidation, reduces VLDL secretion, and raises HDL.*

### 3.7 Bile Acid Sequestrants

34. [Insull W Jr. 2006. Clinical utility of bile acid sequestrants in patients with dyslipidemia: a scientific review. *South Med J*. PMID: 16382702](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/16382702/)
    *Mechanism of cholestyramine/colesevelam: interruption of enterohepatic bile acid circulation upregulates hepatic LDL receptors.*

## 4. Major Clinical Trials

35. [Sabatine MS et al. (FOURIER Investigators). 2017. Evolocumab and clinical outcomes in patients with cardiovascular disease. *N Engl J Med*. PMID: 28304224](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/28304224/)
    *FOURIER: evolocumab reduced LDL-C by 59% and major cardiovascular events by 15% over 2.2 years.*

36. [Schwartz GG et al. (ODYSSEY OUTCOMES Investigators). 2018. Alirocumab and cardiovascular outcomes after acute coronary syndrome. *N Engl J Med*. PMID: 29544982](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/29544982/)
    *ODYSSEY OUTCOMES: alirocumab reduced MACE by 15% and all-cause mortality in ACS patients.*

37. [Cannon CP et al. (IMPROVE-IT Investigators). 2015. Ezetimibe added to statin therapy after acute coronary syndromes. *N Engl J Med*. PMID: 25405393](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/25405393/)
    *IMPROVE-IT: ezetimibe + simvastatin further reduced LDL-C and 7-year MACE vs simvastatin alone.*

38. [Ray KK et al. (CLEAR Harmony). 2019. Safety and efficacy of bempedoic acid to reduce LDL cholesterol. *N Engl J Med*. PMID: 31893580](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/31893580/)
    *CLEAR Harmony: bempedoic acid reduced LDL-C by 18% vs placebo in statin-intolerant patients.*

39. [Ray KK et al. (ORION-1). 2017. Two phase 3 trials of inclisiran in patients with elevated LDL cholesterol. *N Engl J Med*. PMID: 29185996](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/29185996/)
    *ORION-1: single dose inclisiran siRNA reduced LDL-C by up to 51% at day 180.*

40. [Wright RS et al. (ORION-3). 2021. Pooled patient-level analysis of inclisiran trials in patients with familial hypercholesterolaemia or atherosclerosis. *Eur J Prev Cardiol*. PMID: 33624074](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/33624074/)
    *ORION-3: long-term durability of inclisiran LDL-C lowering over 4 years.*

41. [Heart Protection Study Collaborative Group. 2002. MRC/BHF Heart Protection Study of cholesterol lowering with simvastatin in 20,536 high-risk individuals. *Lancet*. PMID: 12114036](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/12114036/)
    *HPS: simvastatin 40 mg reduced all-cause mortality and major vascular events in high-risk patients.*

42. [Sever PS et al. (ASCOT-LLA). 2003. Prevention of coronary and stroke events with atorvastatin in hypertensive patients who have average or lower-than-average cholesterol concentrations. *Lancet*. PMID: 12686036](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/12686036/)
    *ASCOT-LLA: atorvastatin 10 mg reduced fatal/non-fatal coronary events by 36% in hypertensives.*

43. [LaRosa JC et al. (TNT). 2005. Intensive lipid lowering with atorvastatin in patients with stable coronary disease. *N Engl J Med*. PMID: 15755765](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/15755765/)
    *TNT: atorvastatin 80 mg vs 10 mg further reduced major cardiovascular events, validating "lower is better."*

44. [Scandinavian Simvastatin Survival Study Group (4S). 1994. Randomised trial of cholesterol lowering in 4444 patients with coronary heart disease. *Lancet*. PMID: 7968073](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/7968073/)
    *4S: first trial proving statin therapy reduces total mortality in coronary heart disease patients.*

45. [Shepherd J et al. (WOSCOPS). 1995. Prevention of coronary heart disease with pravastatin in men with hypercholesterolemia. *N Engl J Med*. PMID: 7566020](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/7566020/)
    *WOSCOPS: pravastatin primary prevention reduced coronary events by 31% in hypercholesterolaemic men.*
