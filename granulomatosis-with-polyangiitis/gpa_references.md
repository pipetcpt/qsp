## 1. Key Clinical Trials

1. **Stone JH, Merkel PA, Spiera R, et al.** (2010). Rituximab versus cyclophosphamide for ANCA-associated vasculitis. *NEJM*, 363, 221–232.
   - RAVE trial: RTX non-inferior to CYC for induction of remission in GPA/MPA
   - https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/20647199

2. **Jones RB, Tervaert JWC, Hauser T, et al.** (2010). Rituximab versus cyclophosphamide in ANCA-associated renal vasculitis. *NEJM*, 363, 211–220.
   - RITUXVAS trial: RTX+CYC (2 pulses) vs CYC induction; non-inferior remission
   - https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/20647200

3. **Charles P, Terrier B, Tierce JC, et al.** (2014). Comparison of individually tailored versus fixed-schedule rituximab regimen to maintain ANCA-associated vasculitis remission: results from a multicentre, randomized controlled, phase III trial (MAINRITSAN2). *Ann Rheum Dis*, 77, 1143–1149.
   - https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/29352073

4. **Charles P, Perrodeau É, Samson M, et al.** (2023). Long-term rituximab use to maintain remission of antineutrophil cytoplasmic antibody–associated vasculitis (MAINRITSAN3): a multicentre, open-label, randomized trial. *NEJM*, 388, 308–318.
   - RTX 500 mg q6m superior to q18m for relapse prevention; 4% vs 20% relapse at 24m
   - https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/36546673

5. **Jayne DRW, Merkel PA, Schall TJ, et al.** (2021). Avacopan for the treatment of ANCA-associated vasculitis. *NEJM*, 384, 599–609.
   - ADVOCATE trial: Avacopan vs high-dose GC; non-inferior BVAS at 26 wk; superior at 52 wk sustained remission
   - https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/34597417

6. **Specks U, Merkel PA, Seo P, et al.** (2013). Efficacy of remission-induction regimens for ANCA-associated vasculitis. *NEJM*, 369, 417–427.
   - RAVE 18-month follow-up: RTX superior to CYC for relapsing disease (64% vs 53% sustained remission)
   - https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/24131199

7. **de Groot K, Harper L, Jayne DRW, et al.** (2009). Pulse versus daily oral cyclophosphamide for induction of remission in antineutrophil cytoplasmic antibody-associated vasculitis: a randomized trial. *Ann Intern Med*, 150, 670–680.
   - CYCLOPS trial: IV pulse CYC vs daily oral; equivalent efficacy, less leukopenia
   - https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/19451574

8. **Charles P, Bienvenu B, Bonnotte B, et al.** (2014). Rituximab: recommendations of the French Vasculitis Study Group (FVSG) for induction and maintenance treatments of adult, antineutrophil cytoplasm antibody (ANCA)-associated necrotizing vasculitides. *Eur J Intern Med*, 25, 6–11.
   - MAINRITSAN trial: RTX maint vs AZA; 5% vs 29% relapse at 28 months
   - https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/25372085

9. **Merkel PA, Niles J, Jimenez R, et al.** (2021). Adjunctive treatment with avacopan, an oral C5a receptor inhibitor, in patients with antineutrophil cytoplasmic antibody-associated vasculitis. *ACR Open Rheumatol*, 2, 662–671.
   - Phase 2 avacopan data supporting ADVOCATE design
   - https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/29356376

10. **Faurschou M, Sorensen IJ, Mellemkjaer L, et al.** (2008). Malignancies in Wegener's granulomatosis: incidence and relation to cyclophosphamide therapy in a cohort of 293 patients. *J Rheumatol*, 35, 100–105.
    - CYC-related malignancy risk (bladder cancer, lymphoma)
    - https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/17985406
